On Grid

The grid power system, also known as a utility grid or power grid, is an interconnected network of electricity generation, transmission, and distribution systems that supply electricity to homes, businesses, and industries. Here are some key features of the grid power system:

  • Reliable Power Supply: Stable and dependable electricity 24/7.
  • Wide Coverage: Extensive grid network for urban and rural areas.
  • Diverse Power Sources: Mix of fossil fuel, hydro, and renewable energy.
  • Grid Monitoring: Real-time monitoring for high efficiency.
  • Grid Stability: Advanced tech to prevent blackouts.

Off Grid

With our off-grid power system, you can experience the freedom and sustainability of producing your own electricity, no matter where you are located. Explore our solutions to embrace off-grid living and renewable energy for a greener and more resilient future!

  • Self-Sufficiency: Generate and store electricity on-site.
  • Remote Locations: Suitable for areas far from utility grids.
  • Renewable Energy: Harness solar, wind, or hydro power.
  • Battery Storage: Store excess energy for later use.
  • Energy Management: Efficient power usage control.

Hybrid

A hybrid power system combines the best of both worlds by integrating renewable energy sources with traditional power generation and energy storage. Here are the key features of our hybrid power system:

  • Sustainable Energy Mix: Integrates renewable sources with conventional power.
  • Energy Storage: Efficiently stores excess energy for stable supply.
  • Grid Connectivity: Connects to the utility grid for backup power and selling surplus energy.
  • Energy Independence: Promotes self-sufficiency, especially in remote locations.

  • Environmental Benefits: Lowers carbon emissions, supports a greener planet.

Microinverter

Microinverters are essential components of solar photovoltaic (PV) systems. They are installed directly on individual solar panels and convert the DC (direct current) electricity generated by each panel into AC (alternating current) electricity, suitable for use in homes and businesses. Here are the key features of microinverters:

  • Panel-Level Conversion: Maximize energy production efficiency.
  • Enhanced Performance: Optimize output, even in shaded areas..
  • System Monitoring: Real-time tracking for proactive maintenance.
  • Increased Safety: Enhance overall system safety.
